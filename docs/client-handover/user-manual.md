# User Manual

Document status: Client user guide  
Version: 1.0

## 1. Logging In
1. Open the system URL provided by the administrator.
2. Enter your email or username and password.
3. If prompted, complete onboarding or password setup.
4. After login, the system opens the dashboard.

## 2. Dashboard
The dashboard shows summaries and quick actions for modules you are allowed to use. If a module is missing, your role may not have access.

## 3. Account Profile
Use the account/avatar menu to open My Account. Users can review profile information and request or update allowed account details. Restricted account changes are reviewed by administrators.

## 4. Members
Authorized users can:

- Search, filter, and view member records.
- Create new member profiles.
- Edit contact details, preferred language, household links, ministries, tags, and pastoral fields.
- Import member data where role access allows.
- Archive records when appropriate while preserving history.

## 5. Payments
Finance users can:

- Record contributions, tithes, school fees, sponsorship payments, and service-related payments.
- Filter the payment ledger by date, type, member, or method.
- View member payment timelines.
- Create corrections without editing original ledger entries.
- Export reports for reconciliation.

## 6. Newcomers
Authorized users can:

- Add newcomer intake records.
- Assign follow-up owners and next actions.
- Record follow-up notes.
- Link newcomers to sponsorship workflows.
- Convert newcomers into member records when ready.

## 7. Sponsorships
Sponsorship users can:

- Review sponsorship dashboard metrics.
- Create and update pledges.
- Link sponsors to members or newcomers.
- Track pledge frequency, status, reminders, budget capacity, and outstanding balance.
- Review sponsorship case history.

## 8. Schools, Sunday School, and Abenet
School users can:

- Enroll active members into classes or cohorts.
- Track fee plans and reminders.
- Record attendance.
- Track lesson or mezmur completion.
- Promote students when requirements are met.

## 9. Volunteers
Volunteer coordinators can:

- Create and manage volunteer groups.
- Add members to rosters.
- Record service activity.
- Review group totals and missing lead/contact information.

## 10. Parish Councils
Council users can:

- Manage councils or departments.
- Assign trainees and mentors.
- Record meeting minutes and follow-ups.
- Generate quarterly reports.

## 11. Reports
Authorized users can open Reports to view and export module reports. Reports are permission-based, so finance users see finance reports while school, sponsorship, council, or membership users see their relevant reports.

## 12. Support
If you experience an issue:

1. Capture the module, record name or ID, and what you were trying to do.
2. Take a screenshot if possible.
3. Contact the support desk using the process in the Knowledge Base and Support Desk Guide.
